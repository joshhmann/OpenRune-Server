This article is about the boat part. For the equippable items, see helmet.

Helms are a core boat part used in shipbuilding in the Sailing skill. Players can use the "navigate" option of the helm to control the ship they're on, which changes the control scheme from click-pathing to move the player, to a heading selector which changes the boat's travel direction. An "escape" option is also present on the helm, which allows players to evacuate the boat and return to their docked port.

There are helms of each smithable metal that are crafted with bars, as well as a dragon helm that requires dragon metal sheets to make. Helms require specific Sailing and Construction levels to create, and can be attached to any tier of boat. They are built at the Shipyard by interacting with the boat schematics boards.

When travelling through rapids, a boat with a strong enough helm will gain a speed boost, while weaker helms will cause the boat to take damage. Gentle rapids require at least an iron helm, strong rapids a mithril helm, and powerful rapids a rune helm. Adamant helms and higher are also immune to tangled kelp.

Using the Escape Helm function while on board of a ship, makes you abandon ship. If doing this on your own boat, it will have to be recovered from a port. Cargo stored in the ship's hold, as well as any items dropped on board, will be lost.

Materials

[edit | edit source]

Rafts, skiffs, and sloops all have slightly different materials used for their helms, but each tier follows the same basic formula. Across all shipbuilding, each kind of log always corresponds to a particular kind of nail. Regular logs correspond to bronze nails, up to rosewood logs with dragon nails.
