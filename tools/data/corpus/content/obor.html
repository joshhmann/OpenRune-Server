Obor, the Hill Titan, is the hill giant boss residing in his lair, located behind the locked gates found at the western wall of the hill giant area in the Edgeville Dungeon.

A giant key is needed to unlock the gate leading to Obor's lair for the first time, which may be acquired by killing Hill Giants. Subsequent access to the lair does not require a key. The key will not be consumed when unlocking Obor's lair. When the player first enters Obor's lair, they will be in a safe zone, where they must climb down the rocks in order to fight him. Obor attacks with Melee and Ranged.

After defeating Obor, the chest present in his lair can be opened for loot by consuming the giant key, similarly to Bryophyta.

Obor can be fought repeatedly by attempting to open his chest. This will trigger him to respawn shortly after, regardless of whether the player has a giant key in their inventory. This means you can continue to kill him as a method of obtaining more keys, which he drops at a rate of 1/16. However, using the key on the chest is required for the kill count to increase and to obtain the loot from the chest after the fight.

Obor will also respawn immediately upon leaving and re-entering the instance. When changing worlds while in the instanced zone, the player will be teleported outside the instance.

Obor's ranged attack deals 50% less damage when Protect from Missiles is used.[1]

Strategy

[edit | edit source]

Magic (Snare)

[edit | edit source]

Lower-level players (with 50 Magic and 40 Prayer) need to remember to always turn on Protect from Missiles before climbing down and starting the fight. Start with Snare to prevent Obor from reaching you and recast it every time Obor is able to walk again. Use the highest available Magic spell to kill him. To efficiently maintain distance and minimise damage taken, wait for Obor to be as close to one end of the room before Snaring, then run to the other side, casting offensive spells in between. Wait until he walks to the opposite wall again before repeating. Even with this strategy, remember to bring enough food. Obor can do serious damage as soon as the Snare wears off. If you are free-to-play you might want to bring an energy potion as well.

It can be useful to know that Obor does not always keep walking up to melee distance before attacking. This means he might stop walking towards you before standing next to you, giving you the time to snare or bind him without having to run to the other side of the arena. This makes it easier to cast more spells in a shorter amount of time, making the fight more efficient.

Note: It's been observed that Snare does not seem to immobilize Obor for the full 9.6 seconds on occasion (consistently?). If you're a member, try using higher level binding abilities such as Entangle or the Ice spells.

Magic (Tank)

[edit | edit source]

As Obor is weak to magic, this is the preferred combat style for members with at least 50 Magic, 60 Defence and 42 in the other combat stats. You should wear void knight or elite void knight equipment with the void mage helm. Stand with your back against a wall to avoid knock-backs. Pray a Defence-boosting prayer such as Steel Skin. Expect to take more damage using this method.

Ranged

[edit | edit source]

For free-to-play, at least 50 Defence and 60 Ranged along with 40 Prayer for Protect from Missiles are recommended for Obor. Players should wear a rune chainbody as it provides the best Defence against Obor's crush without lowering Ranged attack bonus. Members with 75 Ranged should use a toxic blowpipe as Obor's low Defence level combined with its fast attack speed makes it the strongest weapon to use against him.

Melee

[edit | edit source]

Melee is also a good option, as it is faster than the magic method (snare) to kill Obor, but it will be more dangerous. At least level 50 Attack/Strength/Defence and 40 Prayer for Protect from Missiles are recommended. Obor's slam attack can knock you back, and your next attack will be slightly delayed whether you are standing next to a wall or not. Members can use an abyssal whip or a dragon scimitar, while free players can use a rune scimitar. You'll also want good melee armour with decent ranged and melee defensive stats. If you are free-to-play, you may also want to bring a Strength potion.

Changes

[edit | edit source]
Date Changes
6 August 2025
(Update)

There is now a quick leave option while fighting Obor.

2 July 2025
(Update)

The drop rate of the giant key has been increased from 1/32 to 1/16.

25 June 2025
(Update)
  • Obor no longer requires a giant key to be fought. However, the first encounter with him will still require the key as a means to permanently unlock access to the boss.
  • Obor's drop table has been replaced with a simpler loot table similar to hill giants, with an increased chance at a giant key, as well as a chance of giant bones for members.
  • Obor's original loot table has been moved to a chest within his lair, which is opened using a giant key and can only be opened when he is defeated.
  • Obor now has a 20% elemental weakness to earth spells.
9 December 2021
(Update)

The minimum HiScores killcount required for Obor has been lowered from 10 to 5.

19 January 2017
(Update)

Giant champion scroll added to drop table.

Trivia

  • Obor was originally suggested by Reddit user Mjterp.[3]
  • Obor is acquainted with Kovac, who considers him a good friend.
