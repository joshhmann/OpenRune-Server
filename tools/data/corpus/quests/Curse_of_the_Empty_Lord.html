Curse of the Empty Lord is a miniquest following the events of Desert Treasure I, allowing the player to obtain a set of ghostly robes.

Walkthrough

[edit | edit source]

Warning: This miniquest will take you into dangerous parts of the Wilderness. Beware of player killers, and be aware that you cannot teleport after level 20 Wilderness. This limit is increased to 30 for the following teleports.

Valdez the Explorer

[edit | edit source]
Valdez's location

Wearing your ring of visibility and your ghostspeak amulet or Morytania legs 2/3/4, go to Glarial's Tomb (not the waterfall cave) north-west of the Fishing Guild. Talk to the ghost standing around the tomb entrance.

Note: Be aware that throughout the miniquest the ghosts will not appear on the minimap, and can be difficult to spot due to their transparency.

Surprised that you can see and understand him, Valdez will offer to tell you a tale of woe. Agree to be told to the story, and he will explain how he was cursed to become a ghost. He blames Rennard, a thief that stole the Staff of Armadyl from Valdez while he was delivering it to Entrana for Saradomin. He will give you a Ghostly robe (top) and ask you to find Rennard, offering a hint as to his location.

To obtain the full set of robes, the miniquest will require you to speak to five additional ghosts while wearing your chosen method of ghostspeak and ring of visibility. Each ghost will tell their part of the story to unravel the mystery of the Empty Lord's curse.

The locations you will need to visit are randomized between three possible sequences. Pay attention to the location hint given, as it will indicate the sequence you need to follow. You can speak to him again if you missed it.

  • If he mentions pirates and a shipwreck, Sequence 1 must be followed for the entirety of the quest.
  • If he mentions the thief being north-east of there, Sequence 2 must be followed for the entirety of the quest.
  • If he mentions the thief being south-east of there, Sequence 3 must be followed for the entirety of the quest.

Note: The ghosts will still appear at all three possible locations, but they will only allow you to progress if you speak to them at the location your sequence indicates.

Tip: When visiting ghosts within free-to-play regions of the Wilderness, you can reduce your risk by equipping the ring of visibility on a Members world beforehand, then visiting and completing the ghosts dialogue on a Free-to-play world.

Reward

[edit | edit source]
  • Ghostly hood
  • Ghostly robe (top)
  • Ghostly robe (bottom)
  • Ghostly gloves
  • Ghostly boots
  • Ghostly cloak

Any part of the set can be reclaimed by speaking to any of the ghosts again, in the same locations you were required to visit during the quest. You may also obtain duplicates of the pieces with no need to utilise the drop trick. Note that the other two locations per ghost will not allow you to reclaim them.

Upon completion, you can talk to Historian Minas on the 1st floor[UK]2nd floor[US] of the Varrock Museum to receive 10 kudos and an experience lamp worth 10,000 experience in any skill above 50.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • If any of the ghosts are spoken to without a ghostspeak amulet, the player may say "All your base are belong to us", as well as some other quotes. These are references to famous translation errors in the game Zero Wing.
  • When speaking to Rennard, the player says "I ain't scared of no ghost," a reference to "I ain't afraid of no ghost" from the Ghostbusters theme song.
  • While talking to Dhalak, he will ask the player if they are the "mighty sorcerer that binds him". If you are level 99 Magic, your character will respond by bragging about said skill mastery.
  • If you lose any piece of the ghostly robes after the quest, the first mysterious ghost at Glarial's tomb will provide a replacement.
  • The ghosts located in the Wilderness also appear in free-to-play worlds, as long as the player has the ring of visibility and ghostspeak amulet equipped, and the player can still talk to them and receive pieces of the ghostly robes.
  • Players can speak to the three different versions of any of the ghosts from the three sequences. If they talk to the wrong one, short and funny dialogues will occur with the ghost.
